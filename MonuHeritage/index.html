Famous Monuments

Eiffel Tower - France

The Eiffel Tower is a renowned and historic landmark located in Paris, France. Completed in 1889, it stands at a height of 330 meters (1,083 feet) and was initially constructed for the 1889 Exposition Universelle (World's Fair). The Eiffel Tower is constructed from wrought iron, consisting of 18,038 metal parts connected by 2.5 million rivets, and weighs approximately 10,100 tons.

Christ the Redeemer - Brazil

Christ the Redeemer statue stands majestically atop Mount Corcovado in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. Completed in 1931, this 30-meter-tall statue is an iconic symbol of Christianity and a testament to the artistry of sculptor Paul Landowski and engineer Heitor da Silva Costa. Its open arms embrace the city, offering a message of peace and unity.

Machu Picchu - Peru

Perched high in the Andes Mountains, Machu Picchu is a 15th-century Incan citadel renowned for its stunning architecture and breathtaking views. Often referred to as the "Lost City of the Incas," it remains one of the most iconic and visited archaeological sites in the world.
